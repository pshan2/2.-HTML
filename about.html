A Culture of Integrity Looks Like Emory

The Emory Integrity Project is an initiative dedicated to deepening and strengthening the culture of integrity at Emory. Focused primarily on undergraduates, the project will engage students through a comprehensive program of co-curricular activities and intellectual engagements designed to challenge perspectives, encourage ethical reflection, and promote moral courage and principled action.

Principles

Emory Integrity is consistently and reliably acting with honor, humility, and helpfulness.

Honor is ethically reliable thinking and behavior, which in challenging situations may require moral courage.

Humility is other-regarding behaviors and attitudes, including respect for and consideration of differing viewpoints, along with an awareness of oneâ€™s own limitations and imperfections.

Helpfulness is an interest in and willingness to assist others in fostering their legitimate goals, interests, and aims.

Initiatives

Every year the Emory Integrity Project team will designate a yearly theme for undergraduate students that will highlight some aspect of ethics and integrity. The theme will aid in the selection of the Common Read, as a guide to planning residence hall and university-wide programs, and for use by faculty in courses.

The Student Integrity Advisory Council, an official undergraduate organization that will serve as an advisory body to the Emory Integrity Project, will focus on the development of the culture of integrity and associated activities, including new student-based strategies to promote integrity and programming.

In an effort to bolster integrity within Campus Life, we will collaborate to enhance initiatives with existing programs including:

  • LeaderShape Institute
  • Volunteer Emory
  • Residence Life Hall Programs
  • Sorority and Fraternity Life Workshops
  • Intersections Retreat
  • Flourish Emory and Happiness Boot Camp
  • Sustainability Events
  • Barkley Forum Events
  • Dinner with 12 Strangers
  • Emory Athletics Workshops
